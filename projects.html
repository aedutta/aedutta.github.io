High School

Throughout high school, I was deeply involved in physics olympiads. Below are a couple key contributions of mine as an active member of this community.

Outreach
  • Online Physics Olympiad. This is the biggest international student-run physics olympiad in the world. I co-founded the competition during COVID-19 and currently manage a team of 20+ volunteers. Over 4 years, I have written dozens of challenging physics problems while planning out most of the logistics for the competition.
  • Everaise Academy. This is a course designed to teach students the fundamentals of classical mechanics. I helped design the course content and wrote over 5 chapters of the published textbook on Amazon. While you can purchase the physical copy there, I may be able to give you a free copy if you contact me.
  • Solutions to Jaan Kalda's Handouts. These contain almost 300 pages of solutions and translations to Jaan Kalda's olympiad handouts. I served as the head author for several of the solution guides.
  • Physoly. I directed an online community of 10k+ physics enthusiasts to create resources/translations for physics olympiads. We were official partners with Discord and I oversaw a YouTube channel with 850+ subscribers. This is a great community to join as it consists of several talented members who can give advice and even those who competed internationally!
Handouts
  • Lagrangian Handout. (Last updated 2021). This handout is not meant to provide a rigorous introduction to lagrangian mechanics presented in undergraduate physics. However, it will go through a practical step by step process such that a person who understands the theory and examples presented in this handout will be able to solve olympiad physics problems through the usage of lagrangian formalism.
  • Surface Tension Handout (Partial). (Last updated 2022). Surface tension is a topic that many textbooks neglect. This leaves many olympians confused when given problems about surface tension. This handout attempts to give an introduction to the theory of surface tension as well as giving several examples and problems for the reader to attempt.

This was a particle physics experiment proposal submitted to the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN) during the Beamline for Schools competition. This proposal became shortlisted (top 20) out of hundreds of submitted proposals around the world. I hope that this is a good reference for anyone interested in BL4S.
