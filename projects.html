Handouts & Olympiads
  • Lagrangian Handout. (Last updated 2021). This handout is not meant to provide a rigorous introduction to lagrangian mechanics presented in undergraduate physics. However, it will go through a practical step by step process such that a person who understands the theory and examples presented in this handout will be able to solve olympiad physics problems through the usage of lagrangian formalism.
  • Surface Tension Handout (Partial). (Last updated 2022). Surface tension is a topic that many textbooks neglect. This leaves many olympians confused when given problems about surface tension. This handout attempts to give an introduction to the theory of surface tension as well as giving several examples and problems for the reader to attempt.
  • Everaise Academy. This is a course designed to teach students the fundamentals of classical mechanics. I helped design the course content and wrote over 5 chapters of the published textbook on Amazon. While you can purchase it there to help support Everaise, I may be able to give you a free copy if you contact me.
Research
  • PUEC 2022 Solutions. (Last updated 2022) These are the solutions my team wrote to get second place in the 2022 Physics Unlimited Explorer Competition. In the 2022 competition, we explored the path integral formulation of quantum mechanics and Feynman diagrams.
  • An Analytic Model of Stable and Unstable Orbital Resonance. (Last updated 2021). This independent research project was presented by my colleagues as a poster at the 52nd Lunar Planetary Science Conference, a prestigious international event organized by NASA and the Lunar Planetary Institute. This conference took place in Houston, USA. Notably, we were the youngest presenters among an audience comprising PhD students, postdocs, lecturers, and professors.
  • Channeling Across a Body-Centered Cubic Crystal. (Last updated 2020). This was a particle physics experiment proposal submitted to the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN) during the Beamline for Schools competition. This proposal became shortlisted (top 20) out of hundreds of submitted proposals around the world. I hope that this is a good reference for anyone interested in BL4S.
